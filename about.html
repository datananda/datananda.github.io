about

who is madeleine filloux?

Madeleine Filloux started working with complex datasets nearly 10 years ago at Brown University where she completed an undergraduate honors thesis on identifying disease-causing mutations in the human genome. (Incidentally, this is also when she wrote some of her first “real” code and crashed the lab computer by writing the output of her Perl program all onto a single line instead of adding line breaks.) Documenting the methodology for this thesis work planted the seed for Madeleine’s life goal: to communicate information in a way that is both effective and beautiful. This desire led her to a master’s degree in information science at the University of Michigan where she expanded her interdisciplinary skill set with coursework in human computer interaction, user experience, data science, and graphic design.

Since then, Madeleine has worked with data across diverse domains to deliver insights to a wide variety of stakeholders. Her experience includes designing data driven interfaces for industrial software applications to make them intuitive for end users, developing interactive reporting for an agricultural sales team to optimize their performance and profitability, and creating an aggregated view of an enterprise’s marketing data to deliver consolidated and actionable insights.

Madeleine currently works as a Senior Visualization Engineer at Land O’Lakes, Inc. where she has had the unique opportunity to visualize the sustainability impact of dairy farm enteric emissions (a.k.a. cow farts). She still crashes her computer every now and then trying to push the boundaries of how we represent and communicate information.

what is datananda?

“Datananda” is inspired by the Sanskrit word ananda meaning bliss or happiness.

It reflects our desire to transcend the often tedious and repetitive nature of ordinary data interactions by creating data experiences that are as engaging and enjoyable as they are informative.
